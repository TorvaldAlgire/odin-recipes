Spagetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti carbonara is a traditional Roman pasta dish known for its rich, creamy texture that comes from eggs and cheese rather than cream. The sauce is made by tossing hot pasta with eggs, grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese, crispy pancetta, and black pepper. The heat of the pasta gently cooks the eggs to create a smooth coating over each strand of spaghetti. Simple ingredients and careful timing produce a flavorful dish that feels both indulgent and comforting.

Ingredients

  • 400 g spaghetti
  • 150 g pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Cooking Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat and cook the pancetta until crispy.
  3. Add garlic to the pancetta and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant, then remove from heat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolk, grated cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  5. Drain the pasta, reserving about ½ cup of pasta water.
  6. Immediately add the hot pasta to the pan with the pancetta.
  7. Remove the pan from direct heat and quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture.
  8. Add a small amount of reserved pasta water while stirring to create a smooth sauce.
  9. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.
